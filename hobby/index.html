About Me

Hello! My name is Jaden Peterson; I'm a junior computer science student at the University of North Carolina and a part-time software engineer.

Although the bulk of my time is spent on schoolwork, work, recreational programming, and miscellaneous school obligations, I highly enjoy rollerskating, specifically inline skating.

I began skating in about 2012 at the age of 9. Then, my parents bought me my first pair of inline skates as a Christmas present, which incited my love for the activity.

About Inline Skating

Rollerskating is a family of versatile leg-oriented activities centered around the rollerskate, a wearable shoe with typically three or four wheels. Unlike ice skating, which is confined to the frozen lake or ice rink, rollerskating can be performed on any flat surface, irrespective of conditions.

Rollerskates can be divided into two types: ordinary "quads" whose wheels are fixed in a two-by-two matrix, and inline skates, whose wheels lie in a line (hence the name). The former are easier to learn; the latter are primarily optimized for speed and rigor.

Where I've Skated

Generally, I prefer skating in either traditional indoor roller rinks or outdoor roads either of concrete or asphault. Typically, I reserve the former for social events in which I'm skating with others and the latter for more solo and impromptu skating.

Indoor Skating

I prefer roller rinks because their surfaces are optimized for skating (they're smooth, yet high-friction) and their music and social ammenities enrich the experience. When in California, Skatetown in Roseville was my favorite rink to frequent.

Outdoor Skating

Ourdoor skating environments vary more; although they're usually more dangerous (due to rough terrain, the presence of vehicles, and steep slopes), they can be incredibly fun and are an excellent opportunity to train without having to worry about clumsy children on a public rink.

How I Skate

As my passion for inline skating has been nurtured, the purpose of my skating has shifted from one of recreation to improving my form and exercising. To enforce appropriate form, I most often skate with my hands behind my back (because one should never rely on one's arms for balance).

Why I Enjoy It

Ultimately, I can't precisely explain why I enjoy inline skating and instead can only enumerate aspects of the experience that make it preferrable to others. A few are:

  • Its versatility and performability in many environments
  • Its cost-effectiveness
  • Its speed and thrill
  • Its intersection with dance and music
