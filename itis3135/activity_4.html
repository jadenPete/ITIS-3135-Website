Website Evaluations

Positive Case Study: Google

The Google search engine allows users to enter a search query and browse among a list of potentially billions of results.
The interface is simple, providing—for each result—a link, brief description, and list of relevant keywords.

  • Contrast: Healthy; links are distinguished from the background and relevant keywords highlighted
  • Repetition: Adequate; every search result is formatted the same; upper tabs use homogenous icons
  • Alignment: Strong; the bulk of the page's content is concentrated on the left and consistently indented
  • Proximity: Strong; the most relevant search results comprise the top of the page; helpful quesitions are embedded throughout

Negative Case Study: Yale School of Art

"The Yale School of Art is a graduate school that confers MFAs in Graphic Design, Painting/Printmaking, Photography, and Sculpture; and offers undergraduate-level art courses to Yale College students."

  • Contrast: Too high; different colors are used where differentiation is unnecessary; heavy contrast distracts the user
  • Repetition: The lack of uniformity on the site lowers the speed at which one navigate and destracts from its aesthetic pleasure
  • Alignment: Nonexistent; elements are placed randomly and follow no discernable hierarchy
  • Proximity: Inconsistent; unrelated elements are placed in close proximity; no clear division between sections exists
